# Website value review: Is it good enough for ₹99/week?

Quick audit of what the site offers today and what could make the Pro offer feel more compelling.

---

## What you already offer (strong points)

- **Broad curriculum**: Python, Data Science, PostgreSQL, Airflow, DBT, Redshift, MSSQL, ClickHouse, Metabase, LLM — covers data engineering and analytics in one place.
- **Clear free tier**: ~5% of every course free, no signup; PostgreSQL, Data Modelling, Data Adventure, Learning Path fully free. Low friction to start.
- **Real projects**: E‑commerce warehouse, sales dashboard, marketing pipeline, customer segmentation, A/B testing, LLM from scratch — portfolio-worthy.
- **Interview prep**: SQL, Python, Data Science, Airflow, dbt, Redshift, ClickHouse, LLM, Data Architect — aligns with job readiness.
- **Tools**: Pipeline Designer, Bus Matrix, Data Modelling — practical, not just theory.
- **Pricing**: ₹99/week, cancel anytime, “less than a chai a day” — easy to justify.
- **Founder story**: Builds trust and human connection.

---

## Gaps that can weaken the ₹99 offer

1. **Certifications are underplayed**  
   You mention “assignments & certifications” in pricing and curriculum, but there’s no clear **Certifications** or **Get certified** page. Users don’t see what they’ll get (e.g. “FKTI Data Engineer”, “FKTI SQL Analyst”) or how to earn them.  
   **Suggestion:** Add a **Certifications** page (or section under Learning/Courses) listing: name, which course/project it ties to, what you need to do (e.g. complete course + assignment), and a short “Why it matters for your resume”.

2. **Progress and “my learning” are invisible**  
   If a user can’t see “I’ve completed 3 courses” or “I’m 40% through Python”, the product feels like a content library, not a learning path.  
   **Suggestion:** Even a simple **My progress** (or **My learning**) page: courses started/completed, projects done, certificates earned. If you already have progress in Supabase, surface it in the nav or profile.

3. **Social proof is missing**  
   No testimonials, “X learners”, or “Used by teams at…” makes it harder to trust ₹99.  
   **Suggestion:** Add a short **Testimonials** or **What learners say** section (quotes + name/role if possible). Even 3–5 lines help. Optional: “Join 500+ learners” (or real number) near the CTA.

4. **Outcomes are not explicit**  
   You don’t clearly say “Get job-ready for Data Engineer / Analyst” or “Build a portfolio in 12 weeks”.  
   **Suggestion:** On homepage or pricing: one line like “From zero to portfolio-ready data projects” or “Built for data engineer and analyst interviews”. Add a small **Career / Outcomes** block if you have placement or role data.

5. **No free “win” before payment**  
   A small, completable free milestone (e.g. “Complete Python Sessions 1–5 and get a badge” or “Finish one SQL interview set”) gives a taste of achievement before Pro.  
   **Suggestion:** One visible “Free badge” or “Free certificate” (e.g. “SQL Basics” or “Python Intro”) that appears after finishing the free 5%, and is mentioned on the pricing page.

6. **App and web value are not linked**  
   Download App is in the nav, but the benefit (“Learn on the go”, “Offline access”) isn’t stated next to pricing.  
   **Suggestion:** On pricing or footer: “Pro works on web and Android app — learn anywhere.”

---

## Summary: Is it good enough for ₹99?

- **Content and breadth:** Yes — the curriculum, projects, and interviews are easily worth ₹99/week for someone serious about data roles.
- **Positioning and trust:** It can be stronger. The main gaps are: **visible certifications**, **progress/dashboard**, **social proof**, and **clear outcomes**.

**Minimum changes that would make the offer feel “complete”:**

1. Add a **Certifications** page (what they are, how to earn them).
2. Add a **My progress** (or **My learning**) view if you have the data.
3. Add 1 short **Testimonials** or **Learner count** block.
4. Add 1 clear **outcome line** (“Job-ready for Data Engineer / Analyst” or “Portfolio in 12 weeks”).

After that, the site clearly communicates: what I get (courses + projects + certs), how I’m progressing, and why others trust it — which makes ₹99/week an easy “yes” for the right learner.
